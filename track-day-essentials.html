Track Day Essentials

Everything you need to prepare your car and yourself for a safe and thrilling track experience.

1. Safety Gear

Proper safety gear is mandatory:

  • Helmet: FIA 8860-2018 certified full-face helmets like Bell, Arai, or Stilo provide maximum protection and ventilation for long sessions.
  • Gloves: FIA 8856-2000 Nomex gloves. Padded palms improve grip and control. Brands: Sparco, Alpinestars.
  • Suit: Multi-layer fire-resistant racing suit for endurance events. Single-layer suits fine for casual track days.
  • Shoes: Lightweight, thin-soled driving shoes for maximum pedal feel. Brands: Puma, Sparco.
  • HANS Device: Prevents head and neck injury during high-speed impacts.
Pro Tip: Always check event regulations for mandatory safety requirements. Bring extra gloves and visor inserts for fog prevention.

2. Car Preparation

  • Brakes: High-temp track pads, steel-braided lines, and vented rotors. Consider Motul RBF600 brake fluid for repeated sessions.
  • Tires: Track-ready or performance summer tires (Michelin PS4S, Pirelli P-Zero). Check pressures hot and cold.
  • Suspension: Tighten bushings, check camber, and adjust alignment. Coilovers recommended for serious track use.
  • Fluids: Top up engine oil, coolant, and brake fluid. Replace old fluids before multiple sessions.
  • Battery & Electrical: Secure battery, check alternator and fuses. Track sessions can stress the electrical system.
Pro Tip: Tape mirrors and remove loose interior items to prevent damage or distraction on track.

3. Tools & Extras

  • Portable jack and stands for tire changes
  • Tire pressure gauge & portable air compressor
  • Basic toolkit: spanners, screwdrivers, pliers
  • Cooling towels, water, first aid kit
  • Data logger or GoPro for lap analysis
