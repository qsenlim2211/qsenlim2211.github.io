Track Day Essentials

Everything you need to prepare your car and yourself for a safe and thrilling track experience.

1. Safety Gear

Proper safety gear is mandatory and can make the difference between a safe day and disaster:

  • Helmet: Look for an FIA 8860-2018 certified helmet for maximum protection. Brands like Bell, Arai, and Stilo are track favorites. Full-face helmets with proper ventilation improve comfort and focus on long sessions.
  • Gloves: FIA 8856-2000 certified gloves are essential. Materials like Nomex resist heat, while padded palms improve grip on the steering wheel. Brands like Sparco, OMP, and Alpinestars are widely used.
  • Suit: A fire-resistant racing suit is mandatory in many organized events. Single-layer suits are fine for short sessions, but multi-layer suits offer better heat protection for endurance track days.
  • Shoes: Lightweight, fire-resistant driving shoes with thin soles provide pedal feel and control. Brands like Puma, Sparco, and Alpinestars dominate.
  • HANS Device: High-end tracks require HANS (Head and Neck Support) devices to prevent whiplash during high-speed impacts.

2. Car Preparation

Your car must be fully track-ready to handle sustained high-speed driving:

  • Brakes: Replace with high-temp track pads and check for steel-braided brake lines. Bleed the system to remove air pockets. If you plan multiple sessions, consider track rotors.
  • Tires: Track tires or high-performance summer tires are critical. Check pressures hot vs. cold; small adjustments improve handling significantly.
  • Fluids: Top off engine oil, coolant, brake fluid, and power steering fluid. For intensive sessions, use high-temp brake fluid like Motul RBF600 or Castrol SRF.
  • Suspension: Ensure no loose bushings, check camber settings, and verify alignment for balanced cornering.

3. Track Tools & Extras

Be prepared for minor adjustments or unexpected issues:

  • Jack and stands (or trolley jack) for quick tire changes
  • Tire pressure gauge and portable air compressor
  • Basic toolkit including spanners, screwdrivers, and pliers
  • Cooling towels and first aid kit
  • Data logging device (like AIM Solo or GoPro) if you want to track lap times and analyze performance

4. Personal Preparation

Track days are physically demanding. Proper preparation keeps you sharp and safe:

  • Hydration: Water or electrolyte drinks every 30–60 minutes
  • Nutrition: Light snacks like bananas or energy bars; avoid heavy meals before sessions
  • Rest: Minimum 7–8 hours of sleep before the track day
  • Mental prep: Review track maps and braking points before entering the circuit

5. Track Etiquette & Rules

  • Always obey flag signals: yellow (caution), blue (faster car approaching), red (session stopped)
  • Maintain safe distances, especially in corners and braking zones
  • Overtake only in designated zones and when safe
  • Never drive aggressively beyond your skill level; safety comes first
  • Check event regulations for any car modifications or mandatory safety requirements
